# Serenity Adaptation Plan

## Positioning Fit
- Use Serenity as the most naturally aligned template for Core Point Ltd's consulting profile.
- Preserve its refined corporate structure and supporting inner pages.
- Treat this template as the strongest candidate for a polished advisory website with room to expand into services, leadership, contact, and future insights content.

## Template-Specific Direction
- Visual tone:
  calm authority, editorial refinement, light-and-dark contrast, understated sophistication, leadership-centered storytelling.
- Design memory:
  an elegant consulting website that feels composed, mature, and institution-ready.
- Constraint:
  improve the design without breaking the existing corporate presentation style that already suits the brief.

## Homepage TODOs

### Header and Navigation
- Replace Serenity identity with Core Point Ltd branding, metadata, favicon, and logo use.
- Rework nav labels to support a consulting information architecture:
  Home, About, Services, Leadership, FAQ or Insights, Contact.
- Review sticky header behavior and spacing for longer enterprise labels.

### Hero
- Replace the generic welcome copy with the supplied Core Point Ltd positioning.
- Use a high-trust CTA pair:
  "Book a Consultation"
  "Our Services"
- Replace the hero image with either:
  leadership-oriented photography,
  abstract strategic composition,
  or a strong brand-led graphic system.
- Keep the composed feel of the hero and avoid over-animating it.

### About
- Use the tabbed about section to structure the company story into three clear lenses:
  Who We Are,
  How We Work,
  Why Clients Choose Us.
- Thread the supplied company description and "Why Core Point Ltd" points through these tabs.
- Improve tab titles and body copy so the section feels like executive storytelling, not placeholder brochure text.

### Call to Action
- Rewrite CTA to focus on helping organizations translate strategy into measurable performance and sustainable growth.
- Ensure the CTA feels consultative and credible rather than promotional.

### Services
- Replace all service copy with the six supplied service categories.
- Review service icon choices so they reflect governance, leadership, transformation, and performance.
- Consider this section the launchpad for deeper inner-page service descriptions.

## Inner Page TODOs

### About Page
- Expand the homepage about content into a fuller company profile.
- Add a clear statement of sectors served, leadership philosophy, and transformation approach.
- Use the page to house a more complete narrative without overcrowding the homepage.

### Services Page
- Present all six service areas with fuller summaries and stronger strategic language.
- Group related services logically so the page reads as a consulting practice overview.

### Service Details Page
- Build a reusable service-detail pattern for any one service line.
- Suggested initial use:
  Strategic Advisory or Business Management and Transformation.
- Include approach, outcomes, delivery model, and CTA blocks.

### Team Page
- Replace generic team listing with the three provided leaders.
- Ensure longer bios or credentials can be expanded gracefully.
- Add a more leadership-focused intro to the page.

### Contact Page
- Rewrite around consultations and organizational inquiries.
- Ensure form labels match consulting engagement language.
- Add placeholder space for office location, email, phone, and website details.

## Optional Expansion TODOs
- Decide whether to keep or retire unused blog, portfolio, pricing, and testimonial pages.
- If kept, convert them into future insights, sector perspectives, event highlights, or capability stories rather than leaving template filler content.
- If not needed, simplify the nav and keep the main experience tightly focused.

## Responsive and Polish TODOs
- Improve type scale and paragraph width for long-form consulting copy.
- Refine image cropping and card alignment across inner pages.
- Check tabs, buttons, and nav behavior on tablet and mobile.
- Tighten section spacing so the template feels more premium and less stock.

## Content Risks to Resolve During Implementation
- Need final decision on whether extra content pages will be used at launch.
- Need real supporting imagery for consulting, leadership, and institutional growth themes.
- Need final contact details and any future insights strategy.
