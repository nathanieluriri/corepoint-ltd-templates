# Bootslander Adaptation Plan

## Positioning Fit
- Use Bootslander as the boldest and most visually kinetic option for Core Point Ltd.
- Keep the immersive hero, wave motion, dark sections, and strong section transitions, but remove all app-launch and product-landing cues.
- Aim for a high-authority consulting landing page with a modern, executive-tech edge rather than a startup app showcase.

## Template-Specific Direction
- Visual tone:
  strategic, premium, dark-slate and deep-blue, with restrained gold or teal accents for trust and distinction.
- Design memory:
  the unforgettable element should be a commanding hero paired with layered motion and a disciplined consulting narrative, not software screenshots.
- Constraint:
  preserve the single-page flow and existing animated rhythm instead of rebuilding the page architecture.

## File Scope
- `Bootslander/index.html`
- `Bootslander/assets/css/main.css`
- `Bootslander/assets/js/main.js`
- `Bootslander/assets/img/*`
- `Bootslander/forms/contact.php`

## Section TODOs

### Header and Navigation
- Replace template title, sitename, favicon, and logo references with Core Point Ltd branding.
- Rename nav items to reflect consulting content:
  Home, About, Why Core Point, Services, Insights or Gallery, Leadership, FAQ, Contact.
- Review sticky header contrast and mobile menu spacing to support longer executive-style navigation labels.

### Hero
- Replace the existing app/product headline with:
  "World-class strategic advisory and management consulting company."
  "We Transform Strategy into Performance and Organizational Excellence."
- Remove "Get Started" and "Watch Video" style CTAs if present; replace with consultation-led actions such as "Book a Consultation" and "Explore Services."
- Replace phone/app visual on the right with:
  leadership portrait collage, abstract strategy composition, or logo-led institutional graphic.
- Keep the background atmosphere and wave motion, but tune colors so the section feels corporate and not startup-neon.
- Shorten supporting copy into a clear consulting value statement that speaks to execution, resilience, and measurable impact.

### About
- Rewrite the about block around the supplied company description.
- Break long paragraphs into executive-friendly sub-points:
  strategic insight, disciplined execution, operational transformation, leadership enablement.
- Convert any icon list into the "Why Core Point Ltd" proof points.

### Features
- Repurpose the features grid into consulting capabilities or operating principles.
- Possible cards:
  Strategic Clarity, Execution Discipline, Performance Improvement, Trusted Partnership, Leadership Enablement, Institutional Resilience.
- Remove feature language that implies software functionality or product modules.

### Stats
- Audit whether counters are credible for a startup-stage consulting firm.
- If hard metrics are unavailable, replace the section with:
  engagement model, sectors served, delivery approach, or capability pillars.
- Do not fabricate numbers.

### Details
- Use the multi-row details section to explain service depth.
- Map each row to one or two service lines:
  Strategic Advisory, Transformation, Project Leadership, Training, Change Management, Compliance.
- Replace screenshot-like imagery with diagrams, leadership visuals, abstract operational imagery, or branded patterns.

### Gallery
- Since the gallery source content is not ready, convert this section into one of these:
  firm focus areas, delivery snapshots, sector coverage, or an "Emerging Practice Gallery" with intentionally labeled placeholders.
- If kept as a gallery, mark content as early-stage and avoid fake case-study framing.

### Testimonials
- Remove placeholder testimonials unless real client quotes exist.
- Replace with:
  consulting philosophy, engagement principles, or a trust statement section.
- If a slider remains, use it for service outcomes or firm beliefs instead of fake social proof.

### Team
- Replace current team cards with the three supplied leaders.
- Expand cards to support longer executive titles and richer bios.
- Ensure the layout can gracefully handle only three people without looking sparse or broken.

### Pricing
- Pricing tables are a poor fit for this company profile.
- Replace with:
  engagement models, advisory formats, or "How We Work" tiers such as Advisory Retainer, Transformation Support, Executive Programs.
- Keep the card structure but rename and rewrite to avoid commodity pricing optics.

### FAQ
- Replace the full FAQ list with the provided seven questions and answers.
- Tighten accordion spacing for long answers on mobile.

### Contact and Footer
- Update contact blocks to hold real company channels once available.
- Add a consultation-oriented contact lead-in and ensure the form fields feel suited to advisory inquiries.
- Replace footer copy, quick links, and social placeholders with company-relevant information.

## Responsive and Polish TODOs
- Rebalance hero columns so the right-side visual does not dominate on tablet widths.
- Reduce oversized heading breaks on smaller screens.
- Improve section padding consistency between dark and light sections.
- Check that long bios and FAQ answers do not create awkward overflow.
- Review icon sizing, button radii, and shadow treatment so the page feels more executive and less generic template.

## Content Risks to Resolve During Implementation
- Missing real gallery assets.
- Missing verified client logos or testimonials.
- Missing full contact details.
- Need decision on whether to keep a gallery section or convert it into practice-area storytelling.
