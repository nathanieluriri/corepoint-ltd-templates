# Core Point Ltd Template Adaptation Plan

## Objective
- Rework each downloaded template so it fits Core Point Ltd, a strategic advisory and management consulting company focused on strategy, transformation, leadership, organizational excellence, and performance.
- Preserve each template's original layout logic and strengths instead of forcing a single repeated design system across all folders.
- Improve responsiveness, hierarchy, visual polish, and conversion clarity while keeping implementation practical inside the existing BootstrapMade structure.

## Core Brand Translation
- Position Core Point Ltd as a world-class strategic advisory and management consulting company, not a startup SaaS, app, or product platform.
- Use the attached root logo file `company_logo.png` as the primary brand mark source for all template variants.
- Replace tech-product or generic startup language with consulting language centered on strategy execution, operational excellence, leadership, measurable impact, and institutional growth.
- Treat the brand voice as executive, credible, calm, and high-trust rather than playful, salesy, or overly trendy.

## Shared Content Priorities
- Hero message:
  "World-class strategic advisory and management consulting company."
  "We Transform Strategy into Performance and Organizational Excellence."
- About narrative:
  explain the firm's role in translating strategy into disciplined execution and measurable impact.
- Why Core Point Ltd:
  convert template feature or value sections into strategic clarity, execution discipline, performance improvement, and trusted partnership.
- Services:
  Strategic Advisory, Business Management and Transformation, Professional Development and Training, Project Management, Organization and Change Management, Compliance.
- Team:
  feature Dr. Andy Nkalari, Dr. Uduak Amasi, and Favour Iwok with leadership-focused bios.
- FAQ:
  use the seven supplied questions and answers as the default FAQ source.
- Gallery, clients, careers:
  mark as startup-stage content areas that need either placeholder strategy or reduced visual emphasis until assets exist.
- Contact:
  prepare structure for website, email, phone, and office details once confirmed.

## Shared Design Direction
- Keep each template's original character:
  Bootslander stays immersive and high-impact.
  Multi stays classic corporate and content-rich.
  QuickStart stays clean, approachable, and modern.
  Serenity stays refined, editorial, and leadership-focused.
- Replace startup-product visuals, mobile-app imagery, and software metaphors with consulting-oriented imagery, abstract strategy visuals, institutional photography, or elegant pattern treatments.
- Improve typography choices and hierarchy so the pages feel more premium and executive-facing.
- Tighten spacing, contrast, and card consistency so the templates feel intentional rather than stock.
- Keep adaptive behavior intact and improve breakpoints where text density, hero layout, navigation, and card stacks currently feel generic.

## Shared UX and Frontend TODOs
- Audit every template's navigation labels and anchor order so the user journey reads: Home, About, Why Us, Services, Team, FAQ, Contact.
- Standardize CTA intent across templates:
  primary CTA should be consultation-driven, not product download or app-signup driven.
- Replace irrelevant counters, pricing tables, app screenshots, portfolio demos, and testimonials with consulting-relevant modules or remove them where they weaken credibility.
- Improve mobile-first readability:
  reduce headline overflow, rebalance column widths, tighten long paragraphs, and ensure buttons remain visible above the fold.
- Improve asset consistency:
  logo usage, favicon replacement, image aspect ratios, and section backgrounds should feel like one company even when the templates differ.
- Add a content governance pass:
  identify sections that should remain intentionally light until real client logos, gallery items, career roles, and office details are available.
